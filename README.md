# 🌍 World Happiness Report 2024 Analysis

## Executive Summary

This project presents an interactive World Happiness Dashboard developed using Microsoft Power BI to analyze the socio-economic factors influencing global well-being. By examining data from 140 countries, the dashboard identifies key drivers of happiness such as economic strength, life expectancy, and freedom of choice.

### Key Highlights

* Average Global Happiness Score: 5.53
* Total Countries Analyzed: 140
* Happiest Country: Finland (7.7)
* Lowest Ranked Country: Afghanistan (1.7)
* Strongest Drivers: Economy & Life Expectancy

The analysis reveals significant global disparities in well-being while highlighting the strong relationship between economic stability, healthcare, and national happiness.

# Methodology

The project followed a structured analytics workflow:

## 1. Data Collection

* Imported the World Happiness Report 2024 dataset from Kaggle.

## 2. Data Cleaning & Transformation

Performed using Power Query:

* Standardized country names
* Handled missing values
* Cleaned socio-economic indicators
* Structured data for correlation analysis

## 3. Data Modeling

* Created DAX measures for average happiness scores.
* Calculated correlations between happiness and socio-economic variables.

## 4. Dashboard Development

Built an interactive dashboard using:

* KPI Cards
* Gauge Charts
* Correlation Heatmaps
* Ranked Bar Charts
* Interactive Filters & Slicers

# Analysis and Findings

## 1. Global Happiness Overview

* The global average happiness score remained moderate at 5.53.
* Large disparities exist between top and bottom-ranked countries.

## 2. Correlation Analysis

### Strongest Positive Correlations

* Economy showed the strongest relationship with happiness.
* Life Expectancy closely followed as a major contributor.

### Weakest Correlation

* Generosity showed a comparatively low relationship with happiness scores.

## 3. Top Performing Countries

* Finland, Denmark, and Iceland ranked highest globally.
* Nordic countries consistently demonstrated strong socio-economic stability.

## 4. Lowest Ranked Countries

* Afghanistan and Lebanon recorded the lowest happiness scores.
* Political instability and economic hardship strongly impacted well-being.

# Discussion

The dashboard highlights that happiness is heavily influenced by economic stability, healthcare access, and social security. The correlation analysis proves that strong institutions and higher living standards contribute more significantly to national happiness than individual generosity alone.

The analysis also reveals major global inequality in quality of life, emphasizing the importance of policy interventions in lower-ranked countries.

# Recommendations

* Governments should prioritize healthcare infrastructure and economic growth initiatives.
* Policy-makers should improve employment opportunities and social security systems.
* Countries with lower happiness scores should focus on political stability and quality-of-life improvements.
* Further research can explore cultural and psychological factors influencing happiness.
